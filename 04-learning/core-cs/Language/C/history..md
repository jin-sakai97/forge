**Lecture Notes: A History of C, UNIX, and Computation (1978)**

In this lecture, _Dr. Chuck Severance_ explores the historical significance of 1978 and the co-evolution of the _C programming language_ and _UNIX_.

### **The Significance of 1978**

- **The K&R Book:** The publication of _The C Programming Language_ by _Brian Kernighan_ and _Dennis Ritchie_ in 1978 marked a turning point in computer science (1:18).
- **Evolution from B:** _C_ evolved from the earlier language _B_ at _AT&T Bell Labs_. _C_ was designed specifically to support byte-addressable hardware, moving away from word-oriented memory architecture (1:46).
- **System Co-evolution:** _C_ and _UNIX_ were developed in tandem to create a portable operating system that could move beyond specific hardware like the _PDP-11_ (2:32, 13:50).

### **Historical Computing Milestones**

- **Early Days (1950s-60s):** Computers were massive, bespoke, multi-million dollar strategic assets. Software was minimalist and often custom-written for specific scientific or business payroll tasks (9:15, 10:01).
- **Microprocessor Revolution (1980s):** Personal computers transformed hardware from room-sized machines to commodity chips, allowing for rapid performance growth (11:44).
